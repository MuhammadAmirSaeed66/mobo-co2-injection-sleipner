The numerical model is a simplified, deterministic, Sleipner-inspired test environment used to evaluate the optimization workflow. It was configured using representative reservoir geometry and properties but was not history matched to Sleipner field observations and was not validated as a predictive field-scale model. Consequently, the reported objective values should be interpreted as internally consistent numerical benchmark outputs rather than field forecasts. Geological uncertainty, multiphase-pressure calibration, geomechanical effects, and ensemble-based uncertainty propagation remain outside the scope of the present study and constitute priorities for future work.
